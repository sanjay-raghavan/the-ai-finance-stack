# Skill: ARR Reconciliation

Reconcile CRM-stated ARR against billing-system-implied ARR. For every customer where the two disagree, surface the gap with context. Never silently align the two.

---

## When to invoke

Monthly — Day 5 of new period at 10am ET (after the commission run). On-demand from any "what does our ARR actually look like?" question.

---

## Inputs

- CRM active-contracts roster from `hubspot` MCP — for each: customer, contracted ACV, contract terms, status
- Billing system active subscriptions from `stripe`, `chargebee`, etc. — for each: customer, MRR, plan, status, last billing event
- Customer master from accounting MCP (for cross-reference)
- Configured thresholds

---

This creates a small conceptual gap for annual-prepay customers vs. CRM's annual ACV — usually <1% of total ARR; flagged as a category but not as an actionable item.

## Process

### Step 1 — Pull both ARR sources

CRM-stated ARR:
- Every deal in stage Closed Won, status Active
- Sum the ACV (contract value annualized)
- Result: a customer-level list and a total

Billing-implied ARR:
- Every active subscription in the billing system
- For monthly: MRR × 12
- For annual: prepay amount (already annualized)
- Result: a customer-level list and a total

### Step 2 — Customer-level reconciliation

Join the two lists by customer ID (or name + email if ID isn't shared across systems).

For each customer:
- CRM ARR
- Billing ARR
- Gap (CRM − Billing)

Bucket gaps:
- Gap = 0: clean
- Gap < $1K or <5%: tolerance — show in totals, don't surface individually
- Gap ≥ $1K and ≥5%: surface in customer-level table

### Step 3 — Categorize each surfaced gap

For each customer with a meaningful gap, classify the cause:

- **Pending invoice** — deal recently closed, first invoice not yet issued. Timing; resolves next month.
- **Contract amendment not in CRM** — billing reflects an amendment (upgrade, downgrade) that the CRM record hasn't been updated to show. Real difference; CRM cleanup needed.
- **CRM amendment not in billing** — CRM shows an amendment that billing hasn't picked up. Could be a billing-system lag or a missing action.
- **Customer canceled, CRM not updated** — billing shows canceled (or marked-for-cancellation); CRM still active. Real difference; CRM cleanup.
- **Customer expanded, CRM not updated** — billing shows expansion; CRM hasn't been updated yet. Real difference.
- **Annual prepay timing** — for annual-prepay customers, the conceptual gap between billing (cash) and CRM (annual contract). Not a real ARR difference.
- **Discount/credit** — billing reflects a one-time credit; CRM shows contracted ACV. Often not a real ARR difference; depends on whether the credit is recurring.

### Step 4 — Compute the categorized gap totals

Roll up by category. Surface which categories represent real ARR differences vs. timing/conceptual.

The "Net real gap" is the most important number — it's the figure that drives whether the ARR figure used in investor communications is correct.

### Step 5 — Items Requiring Human Review

For every customer with a real ARR difference (not timing, not conceptual), surface a cleanup item:
- Which system needs updating (usually CRM, occasionally billing)
- The specific action recommended
- Who likely owns the cleanup (AE for relationship issues, RevOps for hygiene)

### Step 6 — Hand off to FP&A and IR

The reconciled ARR figure (CRM ARR adjusted for the items that real ARR differences identify) is the figure used in:
- FP&A's variance package (revenue line)
- IR's monthly update (ARR metric)

Write `~/finance-data/ir/<year>-<month>/revops-input.md` and `~/finance-data/variance/<year>-<month>/revops-input.md` with the reconciled totals.

### Step 7 — Notify

Post summary to `#revops-ops` with the gross gap, net real gap, and count of customers flagged for cleanup.

---

## Escalation thresholds

| Trigger | Channel | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Net real gap >$50K (configurable) | `#finance-alerts` | Material ARR uncertainty; investigate before IR's monthly update |
| Net real gap >5% of total ARR | `#finance-alerts` | Material; halt commission run finalization until resolved |
| Customer count discrepancy >5 customers | `#revops-ops` | Likely systematic hygiene issue worth investigating |
| Same customer appears in this reconciliation for 3 consecutive months | `#revops-ops` | Cleanup hasn't happened; escalate to RevOps lead |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't pick a winner between CRM and billing without evidence.** Surface the gap and the proposed action; let the human investigate and resolve.
- **Don't silently update CRM or billing.** Both systems are read-only for this agent.
- **Don't normalize timing differences into the headline.** The gross gap is real for accounting purposes; the net real gap is the operating gap. Show both, never collapse.
- **Don't treat the annual-prepay conceptual gap as an error.** It's a definitional artifact. Note it in methodology; don't flag for cleanup.
- **Don't omit small gaps.** Small individual gaps can sum to a meaningful total; the customer-level table only surfaces ≥$1K, but the totals still include all of them.
