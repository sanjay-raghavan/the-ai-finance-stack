# Skill: Prepay Identification

Scan the AP staged-invoice stream for invoices that look like prepayments. Propose schedules for high-confidence matches; surface ambiguous cases for human classification.

---

## When to invoke

Continuously — every 4 hours during business hours, immediately after AP Watcher's inbox sweep completes.

---

## Inputs

- New staged invoices from `~/finance-data/ap/<year>-<month>/staged/` (since the last identification pass)
- Vendor master from the accounting MCP (for vendor-type context: insurance, software, lease, etc.)
- Contract references from `notion-finance` MCP (where contracts are stored)
- Active schedules registry from `~/finance-data/prepayments/active-schedules.yaml` (to detect renewals)
- Configured thresholds

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Pull new staged invoices

From `~/finance-data/ap/<year>-<month>/staged/`, every file added since the last identification-pass timestamp. Each file has the invoice metadata AP Watcher already captured (vendor, amount, date, description, PO reference, validation status).

### Step 2 — Skip clear non-prepayments

Some categories are obviously current-period expenses, never prepaid:
- Utility bills (electricity, internet)
- Hourly contractor invoices
- Reimbursement claims
- Single-period professional services

Skip these in the identification pass. (Still tracked by AP Watcher; just not relevant here.)

### Step 3 — Evaluate prepayment signals per invoice

For each remaining invoice, evaluate signals:

| Signal | Weight | Examples |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Vendor type matches a typical prepayment vendor** | Strong | Insurance, software vendor with annual plans, lease, professional services with retainer |
| **Description text indicates a service period > 1 month** | Strong | "Annual policy 4/1 to 3/31", "12-month subscription", "Q2 retainer" |
| **Amount structure suggests prepayment** | Medium | Round multiple of monthly billing; clearly higher than this vendor's typical monthly invoice |
| **Contract reference indicates prepaid terms** | Strong | Linked contract document specifies prepayment + service period |
| **Vendor has prior prepayment history** | Medium | This vendor had an active prepaid schedule in the trailing 24 months |
| **PO description mentions prepayment** | Medium | "Pre-paid for FY2026 service" |

Compute confidence:
- 4+ signals matched → HIGH
- 2-3 signals → MEDIUM (surface for human classification)
- 0-1 signals → LOW (treat as current-period expense; AP Watcher's coding stands)

### Step 4 — For HIGH confidence: propose a schedule

Use the `amortization-schedule` skill to draft the schedule structure (start, end, monthly, accounts).

Surface the proposal to `#prepay-ops` for human approval. Default state: "proposed, awaiting approval."

### Step 5 — For MEDIUM confidence: surface for human classification

Post to `#prepay-ops` with the invoice details and the signals matched. Ask the human to confirm prepay vs. current expense.

Don't propose a schedule until classification is confirmed — premature schedule proposals create noise.

### Step 6 — Renewal detection

For every HIGH-confidence identification, check the active-schedules registry for prior schedules from the same vendor. If a prior schedule:
- Exists and is still active → flag overlap (rare; usually a data error)
- Exists and expired recently (within 30 days) → this is a renewal; note in the proposal
- Doesn't exist → first-time prepayment with this vendor

The renewal note matters because the human reviewer wants to know "is this a refresh of a known schedule, or net-new?"

### Step 7 — Hand off

For approved schedules: write to `active-schedules.yaml`, ready for the next monthly amortization run.
For pending classifications: leave in `proposed/` until the human resolves.

---

## Escalation thresholds

| Trigger | Channel | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Invoice >$25K identified as a prepayment | `#prepay-ops` AND `#finance-alerts` | Material prepayment — surface for explicit CFO awareness |
| Proposed schedule longer than 36 months | `#finance-alerts` | Likely a multi-element arrangement; technical-accounting review needed before approval |
| Renewal at >20% price change vs. prior year | `#prepay-ops` | Flag for human attention; could indicate scope change or contract renegotiation |
| Invoice signals contradict (e.g., looks like prepay AND looks like current expense) | `#prepay-ops` | Surface for human classification; do not propose |
| Same vendor produces a second invoice while a prior schedule is still active | `#prepay-ops` | Surface as potential overlap; clarify before booking |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't auto-classify MEDIUM-confidence cases as prepayments.** Surface them. The cost of asking the human is much lower than the cost of an incorrectly-classified prepayment that distorts the books.
- **Don't propose schedules for invoices AP Watcher flagged.** If AP Watcher's validation flagged the invoice (missing PO, duplicate suspected), wait until AP Watcher's flag is resolved before identification.
- **Don't extend a prior schedule into a renewal.** Renewals are new schedules with new IDs. Continuity is conceptual; the data model treats them as separate records.
- **Don't infer service period from amount alone.** "$24K must be a 12-month service" is wrong if the contract actually specifies 6 months at $4K/month with $24K minimum. Read the contract; don't infer.
- **Don't surface every $500 SaaS annual subscription as needing review.** Below the threshold ($5K default), auto-propose with HIGH confidence and let the human spot-check; surface only the materials.
