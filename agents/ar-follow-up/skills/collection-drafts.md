# Skill: Collection Drafts

Weekly batch of tone-matched collection email drafts. Each customer's draft is calibrated to their payment history, not their dollar amount. Drafts only — never sent.

---

## When to invoke

Weekly on Tuesday 9am ET. On-demand for specific invoices ("draft a follow-up for invoice X").

---

## Inputs

- Today's aging report (from `aging-analysis` skill)
- Customer payment history (trailing 12 months minimum)
- Prior follow-up history (when did this customer last receive a follow-up? from `~/finance-data/ar/<year>-<month>/drafts/`)
- Customer-specific notes (from `notion-finance` if maintained)
- Tone-band definitions and templates from `config.yaml`

---

Per invoice, a draft email at `~/finance-data/ar/<year>-<month>/drafts/<invoice>-<date>.md`:

## Process

### Step 1 — Identify drafts needed

From today's aging report, find every invoice where:
- It's in 31+ aging
- No follow-up has been sent in the past 7 days (or per customer-specific cadence)

For first-follow-up: any invoice that's just entered 31+ on aging-refresh today.

### Step 2 — Pull customer context per invoice

For each:
- Trailing-12-month payment history (count, % on-time, avg days-late)
- Tone band classification (from aging-analysis skill's output)
- Last follow-up sent to this customer (date, tone band, text)
- Customer relationship context (recent expansion, downgrades, disputes from CRM)

### Step 3 — Draft the email per tone band

Apply the right template:

**Good band — gentle reminder:**
> Subject: Friendly reminder on invoice [num]
> 
> Hi [name],
> 
> Quick reminder that invoice [num] for $[amount] was due on [date] and shows as outstanding. Likely just a timing thing — could you confirm payment is in motion?
> 
> Thanks!

**Standard band — follow up:**
> Subject: Following up on invoice [num] ($[amount]) — due [date]
> 
> Hi [name],
> 
> Following up on invoice [num], sent on [issue-date] with payment due [due-date]. The current balance of $[amount] shows as outstanding on our end.
> 
> Could you confirm the expected payment timing? Happy to share a new copy of the invoice or work through any questions.
> 
> Appreciate the help —

**Repeat-Late band — firmer:**
> Subject: Invoice [num] now [N] days past due
> 
> Hi [name],
> 
> Invoice [num] for $[amount] is now [N] days past due. We've noted this is the [Nth] invoice this year that's gone past terms.
> 
> Could you please confirm a specific payment date by [end of this week]? Happy to discuss any underlying issue if there is one.
> 
> Best —

**Chronic band — urgent, still professional:**
> Subject: Invoice [num] — payment date needed by [date]
> 
> Hi [name],
> 
> Invoice [num] for $[amount] is now [N] days overdue. We need to lock in a concrete payment date.
> 
> Please respond by [date 3 business days out] with either:
> 1. A specific payment date, or
> 2. A description of any open issue blocking payment
> 
> If we don't hear back, we'll need to escalate internally. Reaching out directly is the best path to keep things on track.
> 
> Thanks —

Never use legal threats, never imply collections-agency referral, never threaten service termination unless a human has explicitly approved that language for this customer.

### Step 4 — Customize per customer context

- If customer recently expanded contract → softer tone within the band; reference the relationship
- If customer disputed an invoice recently → reference resolution context if relevant
- If a specific contact in CRM is "the right person" → address them by name
- If the company has a specific cadence agreed with this customer (e.g., quarterly billing) → adjust expectations in the message

### Step 5 — Items Requiring Human Review

Flag:
- Recipient address verification (if HubSpot contact >90 days unverified)
- Tone band borderline cases (just above or below a threshold)
- Sensitive relationship context (recent loss of stakeholder, account in renewal negotiation)
- Any reason the AR Lead might want to handle this account personally vs. via the drafted email

### Step 6 — Write the draft file + Gmail draft

Save markdown to `~/finance-data/ar/<year>-<month>/drafts/<invoice>-<date>.md`. If Gmail MCP connected, also write the draft to the user's Gmail Drafts folder for one-click sending.

### Step 7 — Post the batch summary

One Slack post to `#ar-ops` with:
- Total count and dollar amount of drafts
- Breakdown by tone band
- Count of drafts flagged for tone-band confirmation
- Link to drafts folder

## Escalation thresholds

| Trigger | Channel | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Chronic-band invoice | `#ar-ops` | Surface separately; recommend human handles personally |
| Customer history < 3 invoices on first late entry | Inline flag | Default to Good band; flag limited history |
| Dispute detected (per CRM event or email keyword) | `#finance-alerts` | Skip the draft entirely; flag for human |
| Customer recently expanded contract AND now late | `#ar-ops` | Soften tone within band; flag for human read before send |
| Recipient email unverified for >90 days | Inline flag in draft | Block auto-Gmail-draft; force human address confirmation |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't auto-send any draft.** Ever. Even Good-band reminders go through human approval.
- **Don't use "as we discussed" or "per our last conversation" unless there's documented evidence.** Lawyers hate it; customers notice.
- **Don't include payment instructions in the draft (account numbers, routing).** Reference the original invoice for payment details. Including them in follow-ups creates fraud vectors.
- **Don't promote tone bands too quickly.** A single late invoice in 12 months doesn't earn Repeat-Late. Pattern requires multiple data points.
- **Don't blame "the system" or "our finance team."** Drafts speak as the company, not as a junior representative deflecting responsibility.
- **Don't add legal language without explicit human authorization.** "We may pursue further action" sounds reasonable but is exactly the kind of escalation that requires human judgment per customer.
