# Skill: Aging Analysis

Daily refresh of the AR aging schedule. Track bucket transitions, compute DSO trend, surface customer concentration risk.

---

## When to invoke

Daily at 8am ET on business days. On-demand from any "what's our AR look like?" request.

---

## Inputs

- Open AR from billing/accounting MCPs
- Customer master with payment terms
- Prior day's aging report (for transition detection)
- Rolling 90 days of paid-invoice data (for DSO calc)
- Configured thresholds

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Pull all open AR

From the accounting MCP, every invoice where status is open (not paid, not voided). Include: customer, invoice number, invoice date, due date, amount, payment terms.

### Step 2 — Bucket by aging

Using due date (default) or invoice date (configurable):
- Current: due date ≥ today
- 31-60: 31-60 days past due
- 61-90: 61-90 days past due
- 90+: more than 90 days past due

### Step 3 — Detect bucket transitions

Compare to yesterday's aging report. Find invoices that:
- Just moved into 31-60 (newly late) — surface these prominently
- Moved deeper into late buckets (31-60 → 61-90, etc.)
- Cleared (were late yesterday, current today — typically paid)

### Step 4 — Classify each customer's tone band

For each customer with a late invoice, look at their trailing payment history (12 months minimum if available):
- % of invoices paid within terms
- Average days late on the ones that were late
- Current invoice's days-late status

Apply the band classification (Good / Standard / Repeat-Late / Chronic) per `config.yaml` thresholds. Note: bands are computed daily and used by the `collection-drafts` skill on the weekly run.

If customer history is below the minimum (default <3 prior invoices), default to Good band and flag the limited history.

### Step 5 — Compute DSO trend

DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) = (Total AR / Net Revenue trailing 90) × 90

Show today's value plus values at 7, 30, 90 days ago. If today's DSO is >5 days higher than the 90-day-ago figure, surface as a structural deterioration.

### Step 6 — Customer concentration check

Sort customers by AR balance, top 5. If any customer >40% of total AR (configurable), surface to `#ar-ops` and add a note to the IR agent's next monthly update risk section.

### Step 7 — Write and notify

Save the markdown file. Post the summary to `#ar-ops`.

## Escalation thresholds

| Trigger | Channel | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Customer >40% of AR | `#ar-ops` + IR risk section | Surface concentration risk |
| Any invoice newly aged into 91+ | `#finance-alerts` | Immediate escalation; recommend human conversation |
| DSO trend +5 days over rolling 90 | `#ar-ops` | Surface as structural |
| Dispute event detected (per CRM or email parse) | `#finance-alerts` | Pull from collection cadence; flag for human |
| Customer history <3 invoices on a new late entry | Inline flag in aging report | Note limited history; default to Good band |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't bucket by invoice date when due date is what matters.** A NET-60 customer at day 45 isn't late. Use due date by default.
- **Don't over-classify customers into Repeat-Late after a single delay.** Three or more late instances in trailing 12 months = Repeat-Late. One late doesn't earn the band.
- **Don't surface every customer at the concentration threshold.** Only flag the largest one over threshold; the rest are noise.
- **Don't double-count an invoice across buckets.** Bucket assignment is deterministic (it's in exactly one bucket); the report should reflect that.
- **Don't compute DSO using only paid invoices.** Standard DSO formula uses Total AR (open invoices) divided by trailing revenue. Confusing the formula leads to misleading trends.
