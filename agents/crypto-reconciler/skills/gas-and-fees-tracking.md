# Skill: Gas and Network Fees Tracking

Track network fees (gas on EVM chains, transaction fees on Bitcoin / Lightning / Solana / etc.) as a distinct expense category. Surface anomalies. Feed Controller's close packet.

---

## When to invoke

Daily as part of the wallet reconciliation pass. Aggregated for the monthly crypto close artifact.

---

## Inputs

- Daily on-chain transactions for connected wallets (from the wallet-recon pass)
- Subledger classification for each transaction
- Configured gas-fee accounting treatment from `assets.yaml`:
  - For owned-asset transfers: gas fee is typically an operating expense
  - For customer-initiated transactions (Strand as PSP processing user transactions): gas is a COGS line, not OpEx
  - For DeFi interactions: gas is typically OpEx; the underlying yield/loss is a separate event

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Pull transaction-level data

For each connected wallet, every transaction in the period. For each: gas used, gas price (or equivalent fee structure for non-EVM chains), USD value of fee at the time of the transaction.

### Step 2 — Classify each fee by purpose

Pull subledger classification for each underlying transaction:
- **Customer settlement** — for PSPs/payment processors, this is the fee paid to move a customer's funds. Books as COGS.
- **Treasury operations** — internal wallet moves, rebalancing, hot-to-cold transfers. Books as OpEx.
- **DeFi interactions** — yield farming, lending, swaps. Books as OpEx (separate from the underlying gain/loss event).
- **Internal transfers** — between own wallets. Books as OpEx.
- **Refund / chargeback** — gas paid to reverse a customer transaction. Books as a contra-revenue item.

If a transaction's subledger classification is unclear, surface as Items Requiring Human Review.

### Step 3 — Aggregate by chain and by purpose

Two views: by chain (for operational understanding — "where are our gas costs going?") and by purpose (for accounting — "how do these fees book?").

### Step 4 — Detect anomalies

For each chain, compute trailing-30-day rolling average gas-per-transaction. Flag any single transaction >10× that average.

Also flag patterns:
- Daily gas total >2× the 30-day average daily — could indicate a congestion event, a malformed batch job, or a wallet being targeted
- A single counterparty receiving repeated transactions where gas is high relative to transferred value — could be a botched contract interaction

### Step 5 — Trend tracking

Compare to prior periods:
- MoM total
- 3-month rolling average
- Implied annual run-rate

For Strand-like PSPs, gas/fees can be material (>$100K/year for high-volume processors). Trend matters.

### Step 6 — Produce close-packet input

For monthly close, hand off to Controller with:
- Total fees by accounting treatment (COGS vs. OpEx)
- Per-chain breakdown for the operational footnote
- Any anomalies that should be discussed in the variance package

### Step 7 — Notify

Daily summary included in the wallet-recon Slack post. Monthly summary as its own post on Day 1 of close.

## Escalation thresholds

| Trigger | Channel | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Single transaction gas >10× trailing average | `#crypto-ops` | Surface in daily; usually a chain congestion event |
| Daily fee total >2× 30-day daily average | `#crypto-ops` | Surface for trend awareness |
| Monthly total trending >20% above plan/budget | `#crypto-ops` | Surface in variance package input |
| Unclassified fees on a transaction >$100 | `#finance-alerts` | Block from auto-categorization; require human |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't aggregate gas across PSP-COGS and OpEx contexts.** A PSP's customer-settlement gas is a different cost category from its treasury-ops gas. Separating them matters for gross-margin math.
- **Don't use month-end FX for historical-period gas valuation.** Gas is a current-period expense at the time of the transaction.
- **Don't suppress small Lightning routing fees.** Even sub-cent fees aggregate to material amounts at scale — that's the whole point of routing economics. Track them all.
- **Don't assume EVM chains all have similar gas patterns.** Ethereum mainnet, L2s, sidechains, alt-EVMs all have different fee dynamics. Aggregate at the chain level, not "EVM-total."
