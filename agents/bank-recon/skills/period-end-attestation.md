# Skill: Period-End Attestation

The formal bank reconciliation artifact for each connected bank account, produced at month-end. Required for audit. The residual must be zero or the attestation fails — no exceptions.

---

## When to invoke

Day 1 of new period, 10am ET (between Controller's 9am Pass 1 and 11am Pass 2). On-demand for off-cycle attestations.

---

## Inputs

- For each account: GL ending balance from the accounting MCP for the closed period
- Bank ending balance from the banking MCP (statement-period closing balance for the closed period)
- All reconciling items currently open for this account (from the matching pass output)
- All reconciling items from prior period that should have cleared (continuity check)
- Account configuration from `~/finance-data/bank-recon/accounts.yaml`

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Pull both balances

GL ending balance from the accounting MCP for the closed period. Bank statement ending balance from the banking MCP. Both must be as of the same exact period-end timestamp.

If either is unavailable, halt and alert. Attestation cannot proceed.

### Step 2 — Compute initial difference

Bank balance minus GL balance. This is the difference that the reconciling items must explain.

### Step 3 — Categorize all open reconciling items

From the matching pass output, pull every open reconciling item for this account. Categorize:
- Timing — bank-side ahead (bank has it, GL doesn't yet)
- Timing — GL-side ahead (GL has it, bank doesn't yet — checks outstanding, wires in flight)
- Adjustment required (genuine JE needed)
- Investigate (still open, with aging)

### Step 4 — Compute the residual

```
Adjusted bank balance = Bank balance
  − bank-side timing items (will reduce bank when GL catches up)
  + GL-side timing items (will increase bank when bank clears the GL entries)

Adjusted GL balance = GL balance + adjustments to be booked (JEs)

Residual = Adjusted bank balance − Adjusted GL balance
```

This is the math the audit cares about. The residual must be zero.

### Step 5 — Status decision

If residual is zero: ✅ **Reconciled** — proceed to write the attestation
If residual is non-zero: ❌ **Halted** — write the file as an alert, surface to `#finance-alerts`

A non-zero residual means either:
- A reconciling item is missing from your tracking
- A reconciling item is wrong (amount or classification)
- The GL balance moved after you pulled it (someone is editing the GL during close)
- The bank balance changed (rare, but possible if the bank reposts something)

Don't ever "plug" a residual. The math has to work. If it doesn't, that's the alert.

### Step 6 — Continuity check

For every reconciling item that was open at prior period-end:
- Did it clear this period?
- If not, has it aged into the >30 day band? (Escalate per aging rules.)

The continuity check is what catches "we've been carrying this item for 4 months" failures.

### Step 7 — Adjustment JE proposals

For each "Adjustment Required" item, propose a JE. Hand off to Controller's Pass 2 — Controller routes through the standard JE approval flow; QBO Poster (when shipped) actually commits.

### Step 8 — Write the attestation + notify

Save the file. Post summary to `#bank-recon` (or `#finance-alerts` if halted).

Hand off to Controller as part of the close packet.

---

## Escalation thresholds

| Trigger | Channel | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Residual non-zero | `#finance-alerts` | HALT — surface immediately; the close cannot proceed for this account |
| Prior-period item still open after 60 days | `#finance-alerts` | Aged investigation item — escalate to controller for resolution decision |
| Outstanding check >60 days old | `#bank-recon` | Surface — likely needs to be voided and reissued |
| Bank balance disagrees with bank-statement balance for prior period | `#finance-alerts` | Data integrity issue — should not happen; if it does, investigate |
| GL balance disagrees with GL closed-period balance for prior period | `#finance-alerts` | Same data-integrity flag |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't close an attestation with non-zero residual.** This is the most important rule of bank rec. Any "almost balanced" attestation is not balanced.
- **Don't apply a plug entry to force balance.** If you can't explain the residual, surface it. The auditor will ask.
- **Don't reuse prior-period reconciling items as "still open" without re-verifying.** Each period's attestation should re-evaluate every open item.
- **Don't omit the continuity check.** The check is what catches multi-month drift.
- **Don't propose adjusting JEs without the Controller seeing them.** Bank Recon proposes; Controller routes through approval; Poster commits.
