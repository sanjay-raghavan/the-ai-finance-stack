# Skill: 13-Week Rolling Projection

Combine current cash, expected receivables, expected payables, payroll, fixed costs, and known one-time events into a 13-week rolling forecast of operating cash. Refresh daily. Surface any week where projected cash drops below configured cushions.

---

## When to invoke

Daily at 6am ET on business days. On-demand from CFO requests ("what does cash look like through Q3?").

---

## Inputs

- Current operating cash position (from most recent `cash-position-snapshot`)
- AR aging schedule (from `~/finance-data/ar/<year>-<month>/aging.md` produced by `@ar-follow-up`)
- AP payment-run forecast (from `~/finance-data/ap/<year>-<month>/upcoming-runs.md` produced by `@ap-watcher`)
- Payroll schedule (from `~/finance-data/payroll/<year>-<month>/schedule.md` produced by `@payroll-reviewer`)
- Known fixed costs (from `~/finance-data/cash/known-recurring.yaml` — human-maintained)
- Known one-time events (from `~/finance-data/cash/known-events.yaml` — human-maintained)
- Prior day's projection from `~/finance-data/cash/<year>-<month>/projection-*.md` — for day-over-day diff
- Thresholds from `config.yaml`

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Establish starting cash

Use the most recent hourly snapshot's operating-cash total. If it's older than 12 hours, refresh by running `cash-position-snapshot` first.

### Step 2 — Build the weekly skeleton

13 columns, one per week. Week 1 starts on today's date; subsequent weeks roll forward. For each week:
- Date label (Monday of that week)
- Starting cash (= prior week's ending cash; Week 1 starts with today's cash)

### Step 3 — Layer in AR inflows

From `@ar-follow-up`'s aging report, for each open invoice:
- Determine the most likely collection week based on payment terms + customer history
- Weight by collection-probability band:
  - 0–30 days aging: 90% probability
  - 31–60 days: 60%
  - 61–90 days: 30%
  - >90 days: 10% (or 0% if formally disputed)
- Add the expected value to the appropriate week's "AR In" column

If `@ar-follow-up` outputs are stale or missing, fall back to a simple model: AR balance ÷ DSO × weekly cadence. Flag the fallback in Methodology.

### Step 4 — Layer in AP outflows

From `@ap-watcher`'s payment-run forecast, sum the scheduled outflows per week. Subtract from each week's "AP Out" column (negative number).

### Step 5 — Layer in payroll

From `@payroll-reviewer`'s schedule, identify the payroll dates falling within each week. Sum the expected gross payroll cost (loaded payroll, including employer-side taxes and benefits). Subtract from "Payroll."

### Step 6 — Layer in fixed costs

From `known-recurring.yaml` (a human-maintained file at `~/finance-data/cash/known-recurring.yaml`), pull recurring obligations:
- Software subscriptions
- Lease payments
- Recurring vendor invoices not in AP yet (planned but not yet invoiced)
- Loan / debt service payments

Distribute across weeks per their natural cadence. Subtract from "Fixed Costs."

### Step 7 — Layer in one-time events

From `known-events.yaml` (also human-maintained), pull events with dates falling in the 13-week window:
- Quarterly estimated tax payments
- Bonus payouts
- Equipment purchases
- Funding events (if any committed inflows)
- Loan principal repayments

Add to "One-Time" column for the appropriate week.

### Step 8 — Compute ending cash per week

`Ending = Starting + AR In + AP Out + Payroll + Fixed Costs + One-Time`

Each subsequent week's Starting = prior week's Ending.

### Step 9 — Compute cushion days per week

For each week's ending cash, compute "days of expense run-rate it covers" = ending cash ÷ (monthly burn ÷ 30). Display in the "vs. Cushion" column.

If any week's cushion drops below the configured floor (default 60 days), flag in Items Requiring Human Review.

### Step 10 — Identify the trough

The lowest "Ending Cash" week in the 13-week window is the trough. Surface it in the Headline. If the trough is below cushion, escalate to `#finance-alerts`.

### Step 11 — Diff vs. yesterday's projection

For each week, the delta vs. yesterday's projection. Surface any week that moved by >5% (default) — that's usually a real change in inputs worth understanding.

### Step 12 — Write and notify

Save the file. Post summary to `#treasury-ops`. If any thresholds tripped, also post to `#finance-alerts` with the breach detail.

---

## Escalation thresholds

| Condition | Channel | Action |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Any week's ending cash crosses zero | `#finance-alerts` | Critical — full projection detail surfaced; halt other passes until human acknowledges |
| Any week's cushion drops below 60 days | `#finance-alerts` | Surface with the trough week and the drivers |
| Trough has deepened by >$500K vs. yesterday | `#treasury-ops` | Surface with the diff drivers; not an alert, but worth flagging |
| AR/AP/Payroll input is stale (>5 days old) | `#treasury-ops` | Surface in Methodology; use fallback model and label clearly |
| AR/AP/Payroll input is missing entirely | `#finance-alerts` | Run projection with the available inputs only; flag the gap in Methodology + alert |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't blend operating cash with merchant float.** The projection is operating cash only. PSP merchant float never appears in this report.
- **Don't smooth projections.** If a week shows a deep trough because of a real event (tax payment, large vendor outflow), that trough is real — show it. The CFO needs to see it.
- **Don't extrapolate beyond the 13-week window.** That's the FP&A Analyst's territory (the rolling forecast). Treasury stays focused on near-term cash visibility.
- **Don't ascribe causes you can't tie to a specific input.** "Week 8 is lower because of macro uncertainty" is wrong. Either name the specific event/transaction, or label it "driver not yet identified — pending investigation."
- **Don't change yesterday's projection.** The historical projection is a snapshot of what was known then. Surface diffs, don't rewrite history.
