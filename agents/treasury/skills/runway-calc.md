# Skill: Runway Calculation

Compute runway as current operating cash divided by net monthly cash burn, with confidence bands derived from burn-rate variability. Never a single point estimate. Never includes merchant float.

---

## When to invoke

Weekly — Friday 4pm ET. On-demand from CFO requests ("what's our runway right now?").

---

## Inputs

- Current operating cash (from most recent `cash-position-snapshot` — operating only, exclude merchant float and restricted cash)
- Trailing 3 months of monthly net cash burn (operating cash burn rate from the accounting MCP or computed from cash snapshots)
- Trailing 12 months of net revenue (for the optional revenue-scaling upper bound)
- FP&A Analyst's latest forecast (from `~/finance-data/forecasts/`) — for the revenue-growth-adjusted upper bound
- Configured thresholds from `config.yaml`

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Pull current operating cash

From the most recent hourly snapshot. Must be operating cash only — explicitly subtract merchant float and restricted cash. Show the subtraction in the calculation table for audit clarity.

### Step 2 — Compute trailing-period net burn

For each of the trailing 3 months, compute: (operating cash at month start) − (operating cash at month end), adjusted for any explicit transfers in from non-operating sources (e.g., a deliberate sweep from a treasury account, a financing event).

The result is **net** burn — burn already net of revenue. Don't double-count revenue.

Compute:
- `avg_burn_3mo` = mean of those 3 monthly figures
- `worst_burn_3mo` = max of those (highest burn = lowest runway)
- `best_burn_3mo` = min of those (lowest burn = highest runway)

### Step 3 — Compute base runway

`Base runway (months) = current_operating_cash / avg_burn_3mo`

Round to nearest month. If the answer is below 6 months, halt and escalate as critical (see Escalation Thresholds below).

### Step 4 — Compute confidence bands

- `Lower bound = current_operating_cash / worst_burn_3mo`
- `Upper bound = current_operating_cash / best_burn_3mo`

These represent the runway if the trailing-period worst month or best month repeated indefinitely. Honest, not optimistic.

### Step 5 — Optional revenue-scaling upper bound

If FP&A's most recent forecast is available, also compute an alternative upper bound: runway if net burn declines per the FP&A Base case (because revenue is scaling).

This produces a third figure — present it separately from the variability-based upper bound, clearly labeled. The CFO should see both:
- "Range under current burn pace: 16–25 months"
- "Under Base case revenue scaling: extends to ~29 months by month 12"

### Step 6 — Compare to thresholds

Compute distance (in months) from base runway to:
- Bear-case critical threshold (6 months)
- Fundraising trigger threshold (12 months)
- Comfortable cushion threshold (18 months)

### Step 7 — Trend comparison

Pull the prior 4 weekly runway reports. Show base runway week-over-week. Flag any compression of >2 months over a 4-week window as a structural change worth investigating.

### Step 8 — Write and notify

Save the report. Post to `#treasury-ops`. If runway is below the critical threshold, post to `#finance-alerts` instead.

## Escalation thresholds

| Condition | Channel | Action |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Base runway < 6 months | `#finance-alerts` | CRITICAL — surface immediately; include in next IR draft as a flagged item; coordinate with FP&A for scenario refresh |
| Base runway between 6 and 12 months | `#finance-alerts` | Fundraise-trigger warning; surface in Items Requiring Human Review |
| Base runway compressed by >2 months over trailing 4 weeks | `#treasury-ops` | Structural-trend flag; surface in trend section |
| Lower bound (worst-month) below 6 months even if base is above | `#treasury-ops` | Variability flag; means a single bad month could push the company into critical territory |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Never use gross funding raised instead of current cash.** "27 months on the $65M Series C" is wrong when it's been 17 months since the raise. Use what you have today.
- **Never include merchant float in the cash figure.** It's not yours. It's never yours. It can't fund payroll.
- **Never present runway as a single point estimate.** The CFO needs the range. If you're forced to pick one number, use the base (avg-burn) figure, but always show the range alongside.
- **Never use a burn window that flatters the picture.** If the last 3 months were unusually light, the trailing-3-month average understates burn. If they were unusually heavy, it overstates. Note the window choice in Methodology and flag if the window contains unusual one-time events.
- **Never smooth the burn variability away.** The whole point of the confidence bands is to show that runway depends on which month you assume.
- **Never compute runway "going forward" using planned revenue without labeling it as a plan-dependent figure.** Plans slip. The variability-based range uses what actually happened.

---

## A note on how the IR agent uses this

The Investor Relations agent reads the most recent runway report as input to the monthly investor update. The figure that appears in investor communications is always the **base runway** with the range presented alongside — never the lower bound alone (too pessimistic), never the upper bound alone (too rosy), never a single point. This is one of the most-scrutinized numbers in any update; getting it right matters.
